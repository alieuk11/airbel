What is Airbel?

The Airbel Impact Lab at IRC is a team of researchers, strategists and innovators committed to the accelerated design, rigorous evaluation and cost-effective scaling of the most impactful solutions supporting people affected by crisis.

By bringing together field staff, designers, strategists, researchers and technical experts, we aim to uncover and nurture ideas that make a big impact on people’s safety, health, education, income and power.

Innovation has always been the spirit of the International Rescue Committee. The early IRC — founded in 1933 at the call of Albert Einstein — invented new ways to help people escape the horrors of World War II. Today, our commitment to research and development is pioneering changes in products and processes that, in turn, are changing lives.

“Impact is the connective thread between teams’ objectives: to achieve large scale, cost-effective, life-changing impact. It speaks to the mission and purpose in the name. Labs speaks to the expert & experimental nature of what the team pursues: exploration, trial and error, use of evidence, technical and behavioral science, precision.

Jeannie Annan
